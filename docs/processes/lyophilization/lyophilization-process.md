# Lyophilization (Freeze-Drying) — Process Description

## 1. Process Overview

Lyophilization is a three-stage drying process that removes water from peptide solutions by sublimation and desorption under vacuum. The process preserves peptide structure and enables long-term stability.

## 2. Three-Stage Process

| Stage | Purpose | Temperature | Pressure | Duration |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Freezing | Solidify water | −40 °C | Atmospheric | 2 – 4 h |
| Primary Drying | Sublime ice | −10 °C | 0.05 – 0.1 mbar | 12 – 36 h |
| Secondary Drying | Desorb bound water | +25 °C | <0.02 mbar | 4 – 8 h |

## 3. Critical Process Parameters

| Parameter | Definition | Control Range |
|---|---|---|
| Eutectic Temperature (Te) | Temperature at which frozen solution melts | Must remain below Te during primary drying |
| Collapse Temperature (Tc) | Temperature above which cake structure collapses | ≥ Tc + 2 °C margin |
| Glass Transition (Tg') | Temperature of amorphous phase transition | Typically −30 to −10 °C for peptides |
| Chamber Pressure | Pressure during primary drying | 0.05 – 0.1 mbar |

## 4. Residual Moisture Target

| Application | Target Moisture | Method |
|---|---|---|
| Bulk peptide | ≤1.0% | Karl Fischer titration |
| Lyophilized formulation | ≤0.5% | Karl Fischer titration |
