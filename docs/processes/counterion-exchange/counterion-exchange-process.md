# Counterion Exchange Process — TFA to Acetate/HCl Salt Conversion

## 1. Background

Peptides purified using TFA-containing mobile phases exist as TFA salts. For some applications, TFA must be exchanged to acetate or hydrochloride.

## 2. Methods

| Method | Principle | Scale | Time |
|---|---|---|---|
| Lyophilization with HCl | Dissolve in 0.1 M HCl, lyophilize | Small scale | 24 – 48 h |
| Ion-exchange chromatography | Pass through acetate-form resin | Prep scale | 2 – 4 h |
| Dialysis | Dialyze against acetate buffer | Sensitive peptides | 4 – 8 h |

## 3. Acetate Exchange Protocol

1. Dissolve peptide in 10% acetic acid (10 mg/mL)
2. Freeze and lyophilize
3. Repeat 2 – 3×
4. Verify by HPLC (RT shift) or ion chromatography

## 4. Verification

| Test | Method | Acceptance |
|---|---|---|
| TFA residual | ¹⁹F NMR or IC | <1.0% (w/w) |
| Acetate content | IC or enzymatic | Report |
| Chloride content | IC or titration | Report |
